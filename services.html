Our Work

We are committed to making the lives of the needy easier

We make it easy, quick, and safe to support people on the ground who understand needs in their communities better than anyone else.

RakShan Foundation puts donations and grants directly into their hands.

Our Causes

Providing healthcare

We educate and equip communities to stop the spread of life-threatening coronavirus, alongwith diseases like polio, HIV/AIDS, and malaria. We improve and expand access to low-cost and free health care in developing areas.

Growing local economies

We carry out service projects that enhance economic and community development and create opportunities for decent and productive work for young and old. We also strengthen local entrepreneurs

Supporting online education

We support the children that dropped out of school in this lockdown due to not being able to afford the online means. Our goal is to strengthen the capacity of communities to support basic education and reduce gender disparity in education.

The Impact of COVID-19

1.5 billion - children and youth out of school globally.

Over three billion rural workers lost their livelihood.

Lakhs of poor die of coronavirus without being diagnosed due to lack of healthcare facilities.

Our Programs

Step towards making the world a better place

Formation of Women’s SHGs

We have aided in forming women’s groups in various communities. These groups have taken the form of Self Help Groups that have evolved into livelihood generators and as community based monitoring groups of early childhood care centres and the Right to Food (Public Distribution System) in communities.

Formation of Women’s SHGs

Migrant Resource Centre

The centre provides various services and training programmes to informal workers employed in different forms of labour. The centre organises health camps, provides counselling and legal aid, and also aids workers in opening bank accounts and procuring legal documentations.

Migrant Resource Centre

Educational instruction via radio broadcast

We are partnering with local radio stations to broadcast literacy activities. These broadcasts allow parents to support their children’s education at home. According to UNESCO, 75% of households globally have access to radio, so these broadcasts allow us to reach poor students in this pandemic.

Educational instruction

Distance Mentoring Sessions

Social mobilizers conduct virtual mentoring sessions with participants in our Girls’ Education Program and check in with the girls’ families. These video chats and phone calls provide ongoing emotional support, while also monitoring risks that may lead a girl to drop out of school during this time.

Distance Mentoring Sessions

Distribution centres

We provide the necessary food materials, online education school kit, basic medical equipments to the local communities in the rural area. We also organise free food drives for the homeless and poor people to provide them much needed nutrition in this COVID-19 era. For winters, we also arrange blanket distribution drive especially in Northern India.

Distribution centres

Health camps

We also conduct regular health check up camps for homeless and underprivileged who find it difficult to reach to the hospital. In these camps, we provide free of cost COVID tests and medicines to the ones who cannot afford them.

Health camps
